My fork of the official companion repository for *Mastering Kali Linux for Advanced Penetration Testing, 4th Edition*, published by Packt. Security is my professional specialty — I work on cloud security architecture day to day — and understanding offensive tradecraft at a conceptual level is essential to building defenses that actually hold. This fork sits in my account as part of that study shelf.

## The concepts

Kali Linux is the standard distribution for authorized penetration testing and red-team engagements: a curated toolkit for reconnaissance, vulnerability identification, exploitation, and reporting, used under explicit scope and permission. Books like this one matter for defenders too — knowing how an assessment is structured, what attackers look for, and how findings get validated is what turns a compliance checklist into a threat-informed security program. That defender's lens is exactly why I keep material like this close.

## How to use it

The repository itself is light: its main purpose is to accompany the book, and the README's primary content is a link where owners of the print or Kindle edition can claim a free DRM-free PDF copy. For the actual techniques, labs, and tooling walkthroughs, the book and the upstream Packt documentation are the right place to go — I won't reproduce operational detail here, and any use of these techniques belongs inside authorized, scoped engagements.

The fork is unmodified.
